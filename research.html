Research

Research Approach and Weekly Plan

Our project focuses on developing Medimind, an autonomous medical AI for deep-space missions. The research and development process is organized into structured weekly phases to ensure a systematic approach to design, testing, and validation. Key activities include reviewing space-specific medical conditions, selecting appropriate AI models, and testing usability for astronauts in low-gravity and offline environments.

  • Week 1 – Project Definition and Initial Research:
    • Define project goals and scope for astronaut-focused medical AI.
    • Investigate space-relevant medical conditions (dehydration, radiation exposure, muscle atrophy).
    • Review existing AI medical tools and spacecraft constraints, including offline operation and low-gravity usability.
  • Week 2 – System Architecture and Risk Assessment:
    • Draft system architecture integrating wearable sensors and AI software modules.
    • Select AI models including BioGPT for conversational processing and specialized diagnostic AI.
    • Identify required inputs: vital signs, crew-reported symptoms, environmental data.
    • Outline key risks: sensor errors, communication blackouts, potential AI misdiagnoses.
  • Week 3 – Prototype Development and Interface Design:
    • Choose initial AI models for testing.
    • Design user-friendly interface with voice and text input for astronauts.
    • Plan medical test scenarios for early validation.
    • Set up a basic simulation environment to evaluate AI response accuracy.
  • Week 4 – Integration and Testing:
    • Integrate BioGPT with initial diagnostic AI modules.
    • Run test cases using mock health data to validate handling and reasoning.
    • Collect feedback from medical and engineering advisors on usability and performance.
    • Refine interface and AI logic based on testing insights.
  • Week 5 – Analysis and Next Steps:
    • Analyze results and identify areas for improvement.
    • Plan next development phase: additional AI models and real-data testing.
    • Outline safety protocols, training procedures, and deployment steps for future iterations.
